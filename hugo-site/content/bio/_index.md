Nikolaus Wolf is Professor of Economics and Economic History and Director of the Institute of Economic History at Humboldt-Universität zu Berlin. His research focuses on the economic development of Europe, international trade, and economic geography.

He studied economics and modern history in Freiburg and Berlin and received his doctorate from Humboldt-Universität in 2003. Before returning to Humboldt in 2010, he held positions at the London School of Economics, Freie Universität Berlin, Universitat Pompeu Fabra, and the University of Warwick. He was a Visiting Professor at the University of Oxford in 2018–19.

Wolf is affiliated with CEPR and CESifo. His research has examined the formation and persistence of borders, the geography of trade, European regional inequality, national identity, and Germany’s economic development. His work with Gabriel Ahlfeldt, Stephen Redding, and Daniel Sturm on the Berlin Wall received the Econometric Society’s Frisch Medal.
